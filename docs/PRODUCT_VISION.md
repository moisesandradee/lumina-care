# Visão de Produto — Lumina

> **Documento de ideação.** Este documento articula a visão de um produto conceitual. Representa o que o Lumina propõe ser — não o que já está construído.

## Declaração de Visão

O Lumina existe para tornar o cuidado em saúde mental mais inteligente, contínuo e humano — dando às equipes clínicas a clareza de sinal necessária para agir antes, cuidar melhor e se sustentar nesse processo.

---

## O Mundo que Esta Proposta Quer Construir

In the future Lumina is working to create:

- A care coordinator can identify which patients are silently deteriorating before they reach crisis — not through intuition alone, but through structured, evidence-informed intelligence.
- A psychiatrist spends less cognitive energy trying to remember what happened three sessions ago and more time being fully present with the patient in front of them.
- A clinical director can see, at a glance, where the risk is concentrated in their service, how caseloads are distributed, and what patterns are emerging — without waiting for a monthly audit.
- A patient who disengages from care is noticed, not lost — and receives a thoughtful human outreach before the gap becomes a dropout.

This is not a utopia. It is a reasonable, achievable upgrade to how mental health services operate — if the right tools are built with the right constraints.

---

## What Lumina Is

Lumina is a **clinical intelligence layer** that sits between the raw data of mental health care and the clinical decisions that data should inform.

It is:
- **An intelligence amplifier** for care teams — not a replacement
- **A continuity engine** — bridging the gaps between appointments where most deterioration occurs
- **A triage prioritization system** — helping teams direct attention where it is most needed
- **A responsible AI application** — with explicit safety boundaries, explainability, and human authority built into its architecture

---

## What Lumina Is Not

Lumina is explicitly **not**:

- A diagnostic system
- A therapy application
- A substitute for crisis intervention services
- A chatbot for patients
- A tool that makes care decisions autonomously

These boundaries are not limitations. They are the source of Lumina's clinical credibility.

---

## Core Hypotheses

1. **The triage gap is solvable.** Early signal detection in mental health is possible with available data — the bottleneck is analysis capacity, not data scarcity.

2. **Between-session risk is real and underserved.** Most deterioration happens between appointments. Technology can extend clinical reach without extending clinical hours.

3. **Clinician trust requires explainability.** AI outputs in clinical settings will only be adopted if they are transparent, accountable, and overridable.

4. **Ethics and product quality are the same thing.** A system that violates patient trust or clinical safety norms will fail — not just ethically, but commercially.

---

## Strategic Positioning

| Dimension | Position |
|---|---|
| **Primary user** | Mental health clinicians and care coordinators |
| **Setting** | Outpatient, digital health, integrated care services |
| **Core value** | Earlier risk detection, better care continuity |
| **Differentiation** | Ethics-first AI with clinical-grade explainability |
| **Business model** | B2B SaaS — per-seat or per-patient volume licensing |

---

## Success Metrics (North Stars)

- **Clinical impact**: Reduction in mean time from risk signal to clinical action
- **Care continuity**: Reduction in unplanned care gaps for high-risk patients
- **Adoption**: Clinician daily active usage rate
- **Trust**: Clinician agreement rate with AI risk assessments (target: >75%)
- **Safety**: Zero incidents of AI output contributing to adverse clinical events

---

## Long-Term Platform Potential

Phase 1 of Lumina targets **clinical team intelligence**. The platform's architecture, however, is designed to evolve:

- **Phase 2**: Predictive population health modeling for mental health service planning
- **Phase 3**: Research-grade anonymized outcome data for clinical studies
- **Phase 4**: Interoperability layer for national health systems

The long-term vision is an infrastructure layer for mental health intelligence — not a single product, but a platform others can build upon.
